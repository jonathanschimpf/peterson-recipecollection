1 popover pan • 1 ¼ cups - flour • ¼ teaspoon - salt • 3 large eggs • 1 ¼ cups - milk • 2 tablespoons - unsalted butter (cut into 6 even pieces) • 1 tablespoon - unsalted butter (melted)


Oil or spray a popover pan (with nonstick vegetable oil spray). Preheat the oven to 400° and set the rack in the middle of the oven. Preheat popover pan in the oven for about 2 minutes. Blend the flour, salt, eggs, milk, and melted butter until the mixture is the consistency of heavy cream, about 2 minutes. This can be mixed in a food processor, blender, electric mixer, or hand mixer. The batter can be made ahead of time and stored in the refrigerator. Batter should be at room temperature when in use. Place 1 small piece of butter in each cup and place pans back in preheated oven until butter is bubbly, about one minute.

Fill each cup half full with batter and bake 20 minutes. Reduce temperature to 300° and continue baking 20 minutes.

Makes 6 popovers. Popovers are best right from the oven. Serve with butter, jams, or other sweet or savory spreads.
