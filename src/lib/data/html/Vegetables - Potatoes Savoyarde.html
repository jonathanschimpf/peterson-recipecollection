1 Tbs. unsalted butter, melted plus 3 Tbs. unsalted butter, cut into small pieces • 3 cloves garlic, minced • 2 Tbs. finely chopped fresh parsley • 1½ cups shredded Gruyère cheese • ¼ tsp. freshly ground pepper • 2½ lb. varietal potatoes, unpeeled and cut into ¼-inch slices • 1½ cups stock

Preheat oven to 375°F. Brush a 2-qt. baking dish with melted butter. In a small bowl stir together garlic, parsley, cheese and pepper. Layer one-third of the potatoes in the prepared dish and sprinkle one - third of the garlic/cheese mixture over the top. Cover with 1 Tbs. of the butter pieces. Repeat the layers once and finish with the remaining potatoes. Pour the stock and then the remaining garlic-cheese mixture over the layers. Dot with the butter pieces that are left. Cover the dish with a piece of aluminum foil buttered on one side. Bake for 30 min.

Remove the foil and continue baking until the potatoes are tender and the top is brown and crusty, 30–40 min. longer. Serve immediately. Serves 6. Adapted.
