2 packages - dry yeast (dissolved in 1 cup of lukewarm water) • 3 cups - unbleached flour • ¼ cup - olive oil • 1 teaspoon - sugar • 1 teaspoon - salt • 3 tablespoons - tomato paste • 1 medium yellow onion - peeled and chopped (can also substitute green spring onions) • 1 clove - garlic (crushed) • Fresh cracked pepper



1 - Place the water and yeast in a mixing bowl. Add half the flour, ¼ cup of olive oil, sugar, and salt, and mix with an electric mixer until smooth. Add the remaining flour and blend by hand.

2 - Place the dough on a floured board and knead for 5 minutes (this can also be done in an electric mixer with a hook rather than by hand). Place the dough in a large, oiled bowl so it doesn't stick, and cover the dough with plastic wrap. Allow the dough to rise for about 1 hour, or until doubled in bulk.

3 - When it is doubled, punch the dough down, and knead it for 1-2 minutes. Roll the dough out to fit a greased 9 x 13-inch shallow pan, and place the dough in the pan.

4 - Let the dough rise until not quite doubled in bulk, and then punch holes all over it using a fork. Brush the top of the dough with some olive oil and then the tomato paste.

5 - Mix together the onion and garlic and sprinkle over the top of the dough. Sprinkle with the pepper.

6 - Preheat the oven and bake at 375°F for about 25 minutes, or until lightly browned.
