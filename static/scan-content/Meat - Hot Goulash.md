(for 2-3 persons)

4 onions (middle size)  •  ¼ - ½ teaspoon of oregano  •  2 cloves of garlic  •  ½ bay leaf  •  2 potatoes (middle size)  •  ¼ - ½ teaspoon of cayenne pepper  •  3 ounces of spread or vegetable oil  •  3 ounces of tomato paste  •  2 grains of allspice  •  3 ounces of tomato ketchup  •  2 juniper berries  •  10 ounces of beef  •  ¼ - ½ teaspoon of marjoram  •  salt  •  ¼ - ½ teaspoon of thyme  •  mild curry

Time needed about 1 hour.

1 - Dice the onions and the garlic. Peel the potatoes and dice it. Dice the beef, too.

2 - Heat the spread or the vegetable oil in a large sauce pan, and put the onions, the garlic and the potatoes in it. Spice it with the allspice, the juniper berries, the marjoram, the thyme, the oregano and the bay leaf. Fry all until the onions are transparent. Stir constantly.

3 - Add the cayenne pepper. Brown it a short time, then add the tomato paste, the ketchup and some water. Use so much water that the onion are just covered. Stir well again.

4 - Add the beef. Let the goulash boil about 20 minutes or until the beef is done.

5 - Season the goulash with salt and mild curry.

6 - Enjoy the goulash best with rice or bread and a glass of red wine.
